Why Cloud Migration?

Benefits Description Cons
Saving Costs Cloud migration significantly reduces expenses for your business. With a pay-as-you-go model, you only pay for the resources you use, eliminating the need for costly proprietary servers. This cost-efficient approach ensures that as your business operates, you continue to save money compared to traditional data centers. Initial setup and migration costs can be high, and long-term expenses may vary based on usage.
Backup Cloud providers offer robust backup and recovery solutions with just a single click. These capabilities ensure that your critical data is secure, easily recoverable, and protected from unforeseen disruptions, providing peace of mind and uninterrupted operations. Dependency on the cloud provider's reliability and potential issues with service outages.
Scalability As your business grows, cloud resources grow with you. The cloud offers virtually unlimited scalability, enabling you to adjust capacity to meet your needs in real-time. Whether scaling up during peak demand or scaling down during slower periods, the cloud ensures you never overpay for unused capacity. Unpredictable costs due to sudden scaling needs during high traffic periods.
Simplified Management Managing your infrastructure has never been easier. With central management tools, organizations can monitor and control their data center and cloud resources from a single, unified screen. This streamlined approach reduces complexity and improves efficiency in day-to-day operations. Requires training for IT staff to adapt to new management tools and processes.

Perform a SWOT Analysis A SWOT analysis will allow you to look at the issues on hand, and which areas need improvement. Then, you can consider what the cloud would do for you and your business.

Identify Which Apps to Migrate With the SWOT analysis, narrow your focus and prioritize use cases.

Crunch The Numbers Perform a cost analysis. How much are you spending for a proprietary server, and what will it cost you to migrate and maintain your business on the cloud?
